Population information/history

Status:

Vulnerable

Current population:

1,864 in the wild

summary on population history:

Before Pandas were previously considered endangered,
they would be classified as a rare species from 1965 to
1988. For the next four population assessments taken afterwards, Pandas
would be downgraded as endangered from 1990 to 2008.
Things would change on April 11, 2016, when they would be
again downgraded to vulnerable status. While this is a
positive change in direction, the low population of 1,864 pandas
still indicates a struggle in the continuation of the species.

Problems:

Common causes:

Habitat loss & hunting

Overall summary:

Habitat loss is considered one of the main struggles
that pandas face. Specific aspects of Habitat loss are
within the realm of transportation services and
infrastructure which includes:

-Development of roads/railroads
-Wood harvesting
-gathering of plants
